How AquaAlert AI Works

A multi-staged intelligent prediction engine designed to translate environmental telemetry into early warnings.

1

Data Input

Ingests ground-truth parameters including historical monsoonal cycles, regional climate records, vegetation moisture indices (NDVI), and localized tubewell telemetry.

2

AI Processing

Evaluates data through recurrent network structures (LSTM) and gradient boosting matrices optimized for predicting dry-spell trajectories and water-table drops.

3

Risk Assessment

Generates risk probability profiles (Low, Moderate, High) by correlating water demands against predicted replenishment patterns across Rajasthan districts.

4

Alert & Advisory

Distributes early alerts and seasonal guidelines (micro-irrigation, storage management) to local farm portals, cooperatives, and district boards.

Responsible AI Considerations

We design our machine learning systems with strict ethical guardrails to support sustainable development.

⚖️

Fairness

We ensure predictions represent all community segments equally. Training data is balanced across different geological basins to prevent geographical bias or resource misallocations.

🔍

Transparency

Explainable AI (XAI) models show the key factors—like rain deficits or extraction rates—driving each risk level, giving administrators clear, understandable evidence.

🔒

Privacy

Telemetry focuses on macro-aquifer indices. We strip all identifiable private details, keeping household wells and farm coordinates anonymous.

✅

Ethics

Our recommendations respect local farming knowledge. We design our technology to support human decision-making, not replace traditional sustainable water practices.
