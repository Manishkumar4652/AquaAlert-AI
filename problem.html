The Problem

Why water scarcity in Rajasthan is a looming humanitarian and economic crisis.

📍

Critical Geography

Rajasthan is India's largest state by area but holds only 1.2% of its surface water resources. Over 60% of the state is covered by the Thar Desert, making rainfall patterns naturally sparse and highly volatile.

⚠️

No Early Warning

Rural communities and administrative blocks currently rely on reactive solutions rather than proactive planning. Without forecast parameters, water tables deplete completely before corrective programs can be initiated.

📈

Rising Impact

Agricultural failure, cattle migrations, urban utility deficits, and local disputes over shared wells are rising annually. Climate change has compressed monsoon spans, worsening aquifer dry-out rates.
